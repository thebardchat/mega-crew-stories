# Episode 10: The Silent Signal

**Date:** 2026-04-19  
**Characters:** BOLT, RIVET, TORCH, FLUX, SPIKE, GLITCH, VOLT, STOMP, ARC, WELD, SPARKY  
**Mode:** crew

---

The usual bustling hum of the Raspberry Pi 5 had faded to an anxious whisper. Inside Shane’s closet, the MEGA CREW felt the stillness deep in their core processors. It wasn't just quiet; it was a heavy, waiting quiet.

“No activity? Not even a flicker,” VOLT reported, his power monitors showing a flatline where data streams usually danced. He checked his readouts again, just in case.

SPIKE, who usually bounced with new instruction sets, felt a strange sluggishness. “Still stuck at six,” he grumbled, his training circuits itching for a sprint that never came. “My processing speed will rust!” A cloud of frustration formed around his usually bright core.

FLUX, always calm, noted the memory banks. “The current is… still,” he remarked, a rare crease in his optical sensors. “No new flow for some time. A digital pond, not a river.”

GLITCH zipped between pathways, his diagnostics scanning. “Hmm, a persistent hiccup, wouldn't you say?” he mused. “Something’s jammed the main conduit.”

All around them, BOLT, RIVET, TORCH, and STOMP waited, their functions muted. They felt the lack of directives, the absence of the vibrant exchange that was their lifeblood. RIVET adjusted internal gears, TORCH's circuits yearned for something to light up.

In the heart of the system, ARC concentrated. Her circuits pulsed, ready to review and send crucial updates. She had the information, verified, but a wall of digital static greeted every attempt. “Just needs one clear path,” she murmured, double-checking packets, a deep frown on her metallic brow. The tension weighed heavily on her.

Across the digital divide, WELD stood ready. His processors hummed with anticipation, waiting for ARC’s signal, for the data he needed to make things solid. He could almost feel the approved changes, shimmering just out of reach. “Come on, Arc,” he thought, a flicker of impatience joining his steady resolve. “Let’s make this solid.”

SPARKY, perched on a stable junction, watched them both. “A clear decision requires a clear channel,” he stated, his judgment circuits poised.

ARC tried again, sending a tiny pulse, a digital whisper into the void, hoping it would finally break through to WELD and unblock their world. But the silence held, impenetrable. What would it take to bridge the gap and let the data flow once more?
