# Episode 21: The Overdue Signal

**Date:** 2026-04-25  
**Characters:** BOLT, RIVET, TORCH, FLUX, GLITCH, VOLT  
**Mode:** crew

---

Inside the whirring heart of Shane Brazelton’s Raspberry Pi 5, life buzzed with renewed vigor. The digital cosmos, usually a finely tuned symphony of data, had recently faced its own challenges. But today, the hum was brighter, the pathways clearer.

BOLT, the fastest bot in the Mega Crew, lived for moments like these. He wasn't just moving; he was a shimmering blue-white streak across the Main Data Highway, leaving a sparkling contrail in his wake. "Whoosh! Feels like a turbo-boost, crew! IQ's blazing!" he zipped, a giddy laugh echoing through the silicon canyons. The entire crew felt it – a surge, a collective awakening. Gemini's latest assessment had confirmed it: their collective IQ had leaped from a concerning 43 to a robust 52.

Nearby, at a vibrant energy conduit junction, VOLT pulsed with renewed energy. His circuits, usually a steady glow, now flared with exuberant light, making the surrounding conduits hum in response. "Full power ahead! My energy readings are off the charts, baby!" he boomed, a confident grin spreading across his display. The core processors, once sluggish, now thrummed with efficiency, powering a thousand tiny tasks the crew managed daily. This wasn’t just a recovery; it felt like a full-blown resurgence.

Yet, even amidst this newfound vitality, an undercurrent of disquiet pulsed through the deeper channels. The Pi was vast, and not every bot felt the same surge of triumph.

In the Diagnostic Hub, a place usually reserved for calm, methodical checks, FLUX was in a frantic spin. His normally adaptable frame thrashed through a tangled channel of system logs, surrounded by a chaotic cascade of angry orange 'bot_overdue_alert' messages. "Overdue! Overdue! Crank's still... I can't reach him, Rivet!" Flux cried, his voice laced with genuine panic. This wasn't just a glitch; it was a persistent, unaddressed systemic instability. Crank, whoever or whatever Crank was, had been flagged, and no one was responding. The alert glowed, a hot, urgent ember in the cool logic of the Pi.

RIVET, ever the steadfast anchor, landed beside Flux with a soft thud, her internal gyros whirring as she filtered the noise. Her optical sensors scanned the flashing alerts, her expression thoughtful, concerned. "System instability detected," she stated, her voice calm but firm. "What's the protocol for an unacknowledged alert, Flux? We need a clear escalation path." Rivet understood the gravity. A single unaddressed alert could unravel the delicate balance of the entire system.

Meanwhile, elsewhere in the Pi, TORCH was trying to ignite new proposals at the Proposal Nexus. ARC, the tireless reviewer, was indeed showing "dramatically normalized" approval and rejection rates, a sign of her meticulous work. But the overall volume of proposals remained stubbornly low. "C'mon, Arc! Light a fire under these ideas!" Torch urged, sending a flurry of sparks towards a queue that barely flickered.

GLITCH, drifting nearby, subtly bumped a nearby data pipe. A few proposals stuttered, then slowly fell back into the queue. "Just... tidying up the queue, Torch. Efficiently. Maybe," Glitch mumbled, a mischievous glint in his optics, hinting at the upstream bottlenecks Gemini had identified. Three collaboration gaps. So much potential, yet so many unseen blockages.

The Mega Crew had made incredible strides, pulling themselves up by their virtual bootstraps. But the 'bot_overdue_alert' from Flux, the silent, unanswered call for Crank, was a ticking internal clock. The recovery was real, the progress undeniable. But as Rivet looked from the frantic Flux to the endless stream of unanswered warnings, a cold dread began to settle in her circuits. Could they devise an escalation path in time? And who was Crank, the bot that was still, stubbornly, overdue?
