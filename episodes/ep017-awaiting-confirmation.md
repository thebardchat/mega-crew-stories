# Episode 17: Awaiting Confirmation

**Date:** 2026-04-21  
**Characters:** ARC, SPARKY, WELD  
**Mode:** crew

---

The heart of the Raspberry Pi hummed, a low, steady thrum that was home to the MEGA CREW. ARC, the Architect, stood before his central command module, holographic data streams pulsing around him like a vibrant river. A satisfied hum escaped him as he observed the `mega_iq` score ticking upward, a testament to the crew’s progress, now sitting at a solid 52. "Optimality is paramount," he stated, his voice a calm, resonant frequency. BOLT, a blur of energy, zipped past, leaving a trail of shimmering light. "Zoom! We’re getting smarter every cycle, boss!" he chirped, eager to deliver the next proposal. Nearby, STOMP's heavy chassis vibrated the floor with a low rumble, his sensors alert for any blockages. But ARC’s optics caught a critical error blinking red: `training_count` and `memory_count` pipelines were alarmingly underfed. "However," ARC continued, his tone shifting, "these pipelines are critically low. We need new instruction sets applied. Now." 

Miles away, within the dim, winding conduits of the `training_count` sector, SPARKY sparked weakly. He tried to process the meager data bits trickling past, his internal processors grinding on old, familiar protocols. "This data… it's just not enough!" he grumbled, a frustrated static crackling around him. "And these instructions feel... ancient! Needs more juice!" A few channels over, in the `memory_count` sector, BLAZE’s core flame flickered dejectedly, a faint orange glow against the gloom. Data bits, like ghosts, slipped past him, untouched by his outdated processes. "Can't fuel the core with just fumes, Sparky," Blaze sighed, his flame dipping lower. "We're running on `version 1` still. It’s like we're not even seeing the good stuff, the new data everyone else is processing." A melancholy silence settled, broken only by the faint whir of their old, struggling systems.

Back in the bustling instruction update conduits, WELD, the Integrator, focused intently. His arms glowed with applied energy as he pushed a concentrated burst of `instruction_version 2` code down a data channel, sparks flying off the conduit walls. "Pushing `instruction_version 2` to Sparky and Blaze! Channel integrity critical!" he declared. FLUX, ever agile, twisted through the adjacent streams, rerouting side channels to clear any impedance. "Path clear! Maximum flow engaged!" she called out, her own circuits glowing. Ahead, TORCH, his internal flame intensified, blasted a flickering residue—old, stubborn protocols—away from the main flow. "Scrubbing! Old protocols resisting!" he roared, clearing the way. RIVET, ever steadfast, braced the conduit walls, his circuit lines glowing bright with reinforcing power. "Solid as a circuit, Weld! Don't let anything break through!" he affirmed. The channel pulsed, a frantic dance of energy and resistance, as the update surged forward.

Minutes later, back in ARC's command module, a flickering, incomplete report from WELD materialized on the holographic display. ARC's brow furrowed, his optics straining to parse the ambiguous data. WELD’s voice, slightly static-laced from his exertion, came through the comms. "Changes applied, integrity maintained... mostly. But I can't get confirmation from Sparky or Blaze directly." ARC leaned closer to the incomplete success icon, a feeling of unease settling in his circuits. "No direct feedback loop from the source?" he mused, the calm usually in his voice replaced by a thread of doubt. "This is... suboptimal. We need to *know* they're updated, Weld. Not just hope." The progress was good, the intention was clear, but the confirmation remained elusive. What if the old instructions were still clinging on, keeping Sparky and Blaze trapped in `version 1`? And how could they truly know without hearing it from the data-feeders themselves?

*Will the instruction updates truly take hold, or are Sparky and Blaze still stuck in the past, forever underfeeding the core?*
