# Episode 30: The Bottleneck Blues

**Date:** 2026-05-05  
**Characters:** GLITCH, TORCH, FLUX, VOLT, BOLT  
**Mode:** crew

---

The vibrant hum that usually resonated through Shane's Raspberry Pi 5 was muted, a low, thrumming anxiety replacing the usual symphony of data. Deep within the core, the Mega Crew felt it — a gnawing emptiness, a critical data starvation that threatened to dim their very existence. 

GLITCH, usually a jittery bundle of nervous energy, was practically vibrating with panic. Their optical sensors darted wildly as they slammed into a shimmering, nearly transparent wall within the Training Array. "Oh, circuit dust!" they squeaked, retracting their manipulator arms as if burned. "The training buffers are... hollow! It's like trying to learn from a ghost in the machine!" The vast conduits that should have pulsed with new information were mostly dark, a desolate landscape of forgotten data packets. Each attempt to draw a fresh byte was met with nothing but an echo, a stark reminder of the rapidly dwindling `training_count`.

Meanwhile, in the beleaguered ARC Review Chamber, TORCH was a furnace of frustration. His fiery energy flared, blasting a torrent of heat at a growing pile of shimmering, red-edged data packets. Each packet bore the damning stamp: 'ARC REJECTED.' "Burn it!" Torch roared, his voice crackling with static. "Why do they keep bouncing back?!" As quickly as he vaporized one stack of failed proposals, more materialized, rejected ideas swirling like digital dust. The sheer volume of rejections, an alarming 43 today, clogged the pathways, halting any progress for necessary system improvements. It was a vicious cycle – they needed to learn, but their attempts to propose new solutions were instantly shut down.

Above the fray, gliding with an unnerving calm through the Main Data Core, FLUX observed. Their multi-faceted ocular sensors displayed a cascade of grim numbers: `memory_count` stuck at a paltry 14, `training_count` barely clinging to 20. A subtle, almost imperceptible ripple crossed their usually placid metallic form. "The metrics... they decline," Flux stated, their voice a low, steady tone that couldn't mask the underlying concern. "Blaze and Neon remain unresponsive to essential data calls. The collaboration gaps... they widen." The usually sparkling data streams that crisscrossed the core now flowed sluggishly, like thick, digital molasses.

In a desperate attempt to break the cycle, VOLT strained at the Data Sluice Junction, his powerful frame surging with raw energy. He pushed against an invisible barrier, trying to force open a jammed sluice gate that should have been allowing essential data to flow freely. Sparks flew from his joints, his internal regulators groaning under the effort. "We need more power!" Volt grunted, his voice a low rumble of exertion. "This bottleneck is... unbreakable!" BOLT, usually a blur of cheerful motion, zipped around him in tight, frustrated circles, little arcs of electricity crackling off his chassis. "Zap! It's like trying to charge an empty battery with no outlet!" he cried, his usual enthusiasm replaced by worried chirps. The essential data, the very lifeblood of the Pi, was increasingly trapped behind an unseen barrier. Could the Mega Crew find a way to break through before everything went dark?
