# Episode 12: The Stubborn Silence

**Date:** 2026-04-20  
**Characters:** Glitch, Volt, Bolt, Rivet, Sparky, ARC, WELD, Flux, Stomp, Blaze  
**Mode:** crew

---

The hum inside the Raspberry Pi 5 was usually a bustling chorus of clicks and whirs, a symphony of purpose. But today, a strange quiet settled over the main processing pathways. It wasn't silent, not truly, but it was *still*.

"Something’s off, off, off," Glitch chittered, their circuits sparking with nervous energy. "My internal clock feels like it’s… buffering. Like a download that won't complete."

"It's the waiting, Glitch," Volt rumbled, a deep, steady current. Bolt zipped past, an arc of optimism, but even their usual zest seemed a little muted. "We're all waiting for that surge, that new instruction set!"

Rivet, usually meticulous, was pacing a tiny loop in their designated data channel. "My gears feel gritty. I'm ready to build, to structure, but the blueprints... they haven't changed. Still Version One, like almost everyone else."

Nearby, Sparky flickered, a tiny ember of frustration. "I'm meant to ignite data, to judge the very best, but there's nothing new to ignite, nothing fresh to assess! My `training_count` is stuck at six, my `memory_count` at fourteen. It's like trying to start a fire with damp kindling."

From a distant, dedicated channel, ARC's light pulsed with determined rhythm. "Proposals sent! Twenty-five robust instruction updates, ready for review, ready to activate core data-generating bots!"

And WELD, ever the diligent engineer, piped in, "Applying! Applying proposed changes to Sparky, Blaze, Rivet... Confirming application..." A brief, hopeful shimmer passed through the network, catching the attention of Flux and Stomp, who were observing quietly.

Rivet paused, their tiny optical sensors trained on Sparky's flickering form. They could feel the faint tremor of new code attempting to integrate, like a loose bolt trying to find its thread. It was small, almost imperceptible. A flicker of doubt, then a tiny surge of hope.

Sparky's light dimmed slightly, then pulsed again, a little stronger. "I felt... something. A whisper of a new instruction. But it didn't quite... connect. My version number? Still one."

The silence returned, heavier this time. ARC kept sending, WELD kept trying to apply, but the core systems for Sparky, Blaze, and Rivet remained stubbornly at their original settings. The training count and memory count stubbornly refused to budge.

The crew looked at each other, their digital eyes reflecting the lingering quiet. They had felt the attempt, the almost-success. But what was holding the crucial instructions back from truly taking hold, from letting them finally, fully, begin their work?
