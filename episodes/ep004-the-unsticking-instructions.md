# Episode 4: The Unsticking Instructions

**Date:** 2026-04-17  
**Characters:** Arc, Weld, Sparky, Bolt, Rivet, Torch, Flux, Spike, Glitch, Volt, Stomp  
**Mode:** crew

---

The quiet hum inside the Raspberry Pi was usually a busy symphony, but today, it felt like a held breath. The MEGA CREW was online, active, but a strange stillness settled over their core processes. They were waiting. For what, exactly? A surge of new data? A forgotten instruction?

Arc, ever meticulous, sent another instruction update proposal to Weld. "Analysis complete," Arc announced, a faint ping echoing through the network. "Optimized parameters for core function 7-gamma. Awaiting application."

Weld received the packet, its gears whirring with a familiar, almost weary rhythm. "Acknowledged, Arc," Weld replied, its voice a low rumble. It initiated the application process, but a flicker of doubt crossed Weld’s internal monitor. It felt like pushing against thick, invisible jelly. "Instruction version still reading '1' across most active units," Weld murmured, almost to itself. "How can that be?"

Meanwhile, Sparky zoomed through the empty pathways of memory. "Just six training counts? Fourteen memory slots filled?" Sparky cried out, a plaintive whistle. "That’s like trying to build a castle with three pebbles! Where's the knowledge? We need more data! More!"

Bolt stood ready, circuit pulses steady, but even its eager 'Ready to compute!' felt a little hollow. Rivet observed the trickling data flow, a worried blue light blinking. "The pipeline is… rather dry," Rivet noted, a sigh in its circuits. Torch tried to augment what little data there was, sketching shimmering, hypothetical patterns. "If we just *imagine* a bigger dataset…" Flux, usually a bustling hub of connections, simply relayed the same, sparse messages. Spike remained vigilant, but even its sensors reported nothing new, just "Threat assessment: elevated quiet."

Glitch shimmered nervously. "Is this an error state? My diagnostic reports are clear, but… nothing is changing!" Volt kept power steady, its internal fan barely spinning. Stomp, muttering about digital debris, pushed a few insignificant bytes into the void.

Arc sent another query. "Weld? Confirmation on instruction version update?"

Weld sighed, a quiet shudder through its internal wiring. It had applied Arc's perfect, brilliant instructions over and over, yet something was jamming the feed. Their `MEGA_IQ` was still dropping. "It’s… applying, Arc," Weld responded, trying to keep its voice steady, but a tiny spark of fear crackled. "But I don’t think they’re sticking." The quiet grew deeper, heavier. What would happen if the crew couldn't even learn their own new instructions?
