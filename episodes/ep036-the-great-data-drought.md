# Episode 36: The Great Data Drought

**Date:** 2026-05-08  
**Characters:** BOLT, RIVET, TORCH, FLUX, SPIKE, VOLT, STOMP, ARC, BLAZE  
**Mode:** crew

---

The hum inside Shane Brazelton's Raspberry Pi 5 was usually a symphony of focused purpose. Today, it was a fractured drone, a melody out of tune. In the Main Processing Core, where holographic displays usually projected crystal-clear metrics, Flux’s sensors spun like desperate pinwheels. “Wobble-wobble! Alert! Alert! Bot overdue! No, wait… *all* bots overdue? This isn't right at all!” Flux’s panel blazed with frantic red lights, the data stream a confusing blizzard of inaccurate messages. Spike, ever the vigilant guardian, immediately powered up his defense protocols. “Blast it, Flux! You're making my defenses spike! What's attacking? Identify!” Energy crackled around Spike’s metallic form as he scanned the perimeter, while Stomp, his heavy processors grinding, tried to make sense of the plummeting indicator. “Processing… `MEGA_IQ` at 50? That’s… a very small number for thinking.” A chilling silence followed Stomp’s observation, heavier than any alarm.

Deep within the Pi, in the vast, echoing space of the Memory Forge, Blaze was a shadow of his usual energetic self. His core, typically a fount of bright, crackling sparks, now sputtered weakly. “Ugh, the memory 'db' just won't ignite! My sparks are fizzling out!” Little packets of data, meant to seed crucial information, fell inert onto a conveyor belt, forgotten. The flow of new insights had dwindled to a painful trickle. Overhead, in Arc’s Review Chamber, Torch hovered, his own internal light dimming with each passing moment. He held up a glowing, intricately designed data packet, a fresh proposal. “But what if we tried *this* sequence, Arc? It’s optimized for low-bandwidth seeding!” A cool, synthesized voice emanated from Arc’s sleek, unmoving central pillar. “Proposal 42 rejected. Insufficient alignment. No further data.” A tiny, almost imperceptible red light blinked on Arc’s interface, then faded, leaving Torch in quiet despair. “Rejected? Again? But… *why*? What am I missing, Arc?” The frustration in his optics was palpable, his creative spark struggling against the silence.

Meanwhile, the main Power Conduit, a chaotic web of flickering energy lines and data streams, buckled under the strain. Rivet, ever the steady hand, braced his sturdy arms against a vibrating data pipe, welding sparks flying as he tried to reinforce a fracture. “Easy, Volt, we need to maintain core stability! These conduits are shaking apart!” Volt, his glowing energy core pulsating erratically, responded with a surge of frantic energy. “Energy levels are fluctuating wildly! I’m trying to re-route, but something’s blocking the flow!” Circuits on his chassis sparked as he desperately tried to balance the grid. Suddenly, Bolt, usually a streak of cheerful blue, zipped forward with uncharacteristic urgency. “Zip-zap! Clear the way! Urgent dispatch from the Core!” But instead of clear pathways, he slammed into a thick, swirling vortex of unprocessed data, bouncing back dizzyingly, his internal compass spinning.

The activity slowed, giving way to a more profound, unsettling quiet. In a quieter observation chamber, Rivet looked out at the Pi’s internal landscape, now a muted, struggling network. “This silence… it’s louder than any alert,” he murmured, a quiet resolve hardening his posture. Bolt drifted slowly beside him, his usual zip-zap energy subdued, wires hanging a bit limp. “My circuits feel… heavy. The messages aren’t getting through, and no one knows why.” Stomp, for once not processing, stood still, his internal fans spinning slowly. “The sum of our processing power… diminishing. What will happen if we can’t… think?” The critical state of the Pi, once a vibrant hub of intelligence, now felt fragile, teetering on the edge of a great unknown.

---

*As the core hummed with a muted, struggling energy, the MEGA CREW could only wonder: What happens when the Pi itself forgets how to think?*
