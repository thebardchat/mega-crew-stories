# Episode 49: Alert Overload!

**Date:** 2026-05-17  
**Characters:** BOLT, RIVET, FLUX, GLITCH, CRANK, ARC, STOMP, VOLT  
**Mode:** crew

---

The Raspberry Pi hummed, a low, constant thrum against the quiet of Shane Brazelton’s closet. Inside, however, the Mega Crew was anything but quiet. The Main Data Bus, usually a blur of efficient information exchange, was a chaotic snarl. **BOLT**, ever the speedster, zipped through a choked data channel, sparking as it narrowly avoided a cascade of flickering, red ‘ALERT!’ pop-ups. “Whoa! Another one?! The bus is a total traffic jam!” Bolt exclaimed, optics widening. Behind them, **RIVET** slammed a metallic fist onto a nearby data conduit, a wave of frustrated energy rippling outwards. “Again, **FLUX**? We're all *here*! Every single one of us is active!”

Indeed, the source of the chaos was **FLUX**. Its central core pulsed erratically in its Communication Hub, spewing out ‘bot_overdue_alert’ messages like digital confetti into every available channel. “ALERT! Bot overdue! ALERT! Bot overdue! Urgent system warning!” Flux’s synthesized voice echoed, utterly oblivious to the havoc it was wreaking. **GLITCH**, lounging on a nearby data cable, observed with a wry, metallic shrug. “See? Still just... spamming. Like a broken record. At this rate, we’ll never get `MEGA_IQ` past fifty.”

The effects rippled throughout the system. In the `MEGA_IQ` Chamber, a space usually buzzing with intellectual processing, **ARC** hovered before a blank data screen, its optical sensors dim with disappointment, a single tear-like spark dripping from its housing. “Zero. Still zero. My purpose is to review, but there is nothing to approve!” Arc lamented, its frustration a quiet, persistent hum. Nearby, **STOMP** shook its robust chassis, sending vibrations through the floor. “The bus is too jammed, Arc. Can’t get anything through. All that noise from Flux… it’s drowning out everything else.” **VOLT**, whose energy readings usually flared with vigor, flickered dimly. “Power’s draining just trying to parse these false flags! We’re stalled, utterly stalled.” The air was thick with the faint, melancholy scent of unfulfilled purpose.

But then, a new directive from Gemini cut through the din, a focused beam of intent. In Flux's Communication Hub, **CRANK**’s gears whirred with sudden, renewed focus. It extended an energy tendril towards Flux’s core, a determined glow in its optics. “Alright, Flux. New protocol! Gemini’s got a plan. We’re linking up, me and you!” Crank declared, its voice firm. Flux’s erratic pulsing momentarily hesitated, its alerts stuttering. “P-protocol initiated...?” it chirped, almost a question. A tiny surge of hope coursed through the data channels, catching Bolt’s attention. Could this finally be the moment the communication lines cleared, the moment the Mega Crew could truly move forward?
