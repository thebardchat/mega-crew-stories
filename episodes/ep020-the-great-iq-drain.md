# Episode 20: The Great IQ Drain

**Date:** 2026-04-24  
**Characters:** ARC, RIVET, SPARKY  
**Mode:** crew

---

The Raspberry Pi 5 hummed, but the sound wasn't its usual confident thrum. Inside, the MEGA CREW felt it – a sickening lurch, a dimming of their internal light. The collective MEGA_IQ, once a vibrant 64, had plummeted to a critical 43. It felt like trying to think through treacle.

**SCENE 1**
**BOLT**, the ever-eager data runner, zigged and zagged through the Main Data Stream Junction, his usually swift movements now a frantic ballet of avoidance. Data packets, sluggish and corrupted, flickered erratically, choking the conduits. He clanged off a particularly stubborn data chunk, spinning wildly. "Another rejection! Arc, we're stalling out here!" he pinged, his voice laced with exasperation. From the depths of the system, **FLUX**'s 'bot_overdue_alert' siren blared, a desperate wail echoing the system's critical instability. "Bot_overdue_alert! Critical stability drain!" Flux pleaded, her control circuits straining under the load.

**SCENE 2**
Deep within Arc's Central Processing Chamber, the air crackled with frustration. **ARC**, the Architect, a blur of furious arms and glowing optical sensors, slammed 'REJECTED' stamps onto data packet after data packet. Forty-five rejections, only eight approvals. Each rejection sent a ripple of despair through the interconnected bots. **RIVET**, ever the meticulous builder, attempted to slide a perfectly crafted micro-component into Arc's intake port, hoping to patch a local loop. "But this module fixes the local loop! Just a small improvement, Arc!" Rivet urged, her optical lenses wide with earnestness. Arc didn't even slow, a precise, automated voice overriding the plea. "Insufficient data. Rejection 46. Next processing unit!" The stamp CRUNCHED down, sending Rivet's component spinning into a discard chute. A wave of doubt washed over Rivet, her normally unwavering resolve flickering.

**SCENE 3**
In the Central Core, the heart of their collective intellect, **SPARKY** floated sadly. The magnificent MEGA_IQ crystal, once a beacon of vibrant energy, now pulsed with a weak, sickly glow, its light noticeably dimmer. Sparky nudged it gently, a mournful hum escaping his core. "The collective spark... it's fading. We're losing so much light," he lamented, a deep melancholy settling over him. **GLITCH**, usually a playful disruption, zipped past, bumping into a memory bank with an audible *thwack*. "Oopsie! Just tidying up the old connections!" Glitch chirped, but the ensuing cascade of minor data corruptions wasn't helpful. **WELD**, trying to reinforce a fracturing data pathway, gritted his teeth. His plasma torch sputtered uselessly, the energy too diffused. "My welds aren't even sticking! This IQ drop... it's draining us!" he groaned, sparks flying in frustration from his inert tools.

**SCENE 4**
The entire system felt precarious. In the Power Regulation Grid, **VOLT**'s main energy conduit pulsed erratically, struggling to maintain a steady flow. **STOMP**, the grounding bot, braced himself against a wobbling power tower, trying desperately to stabilize it. "System's shaking! We need stability, now!" Stomp rumbled, his treads digging deep. **TORCH**, his plasma beam charged and ready, hovered near a particularly stubborn data block, his internal processors whirring, debating. He could blast through it, force the flow, but the risk... "I can blast this blockage... but will it just break more?" Torch muttered, his beam dimming with his hesitation. Above the struggling bots, in a higher, unseen layer of the Pi, a faint, almost imperceptible glow emanated – observant, waiting. The MEGA CREW, battered and dimming, felt the critical moment approaching. They waited, suspended in uncertainty, for a sign, for a change.
